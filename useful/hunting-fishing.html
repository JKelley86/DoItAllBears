Hunting Seasons & Zones

Deer Hunting

  • Archery: Mid-September to Late December
  • Firearm: Early November to Mid-November (Varies by Zone)
  • Muzzleloader: Late November to Mid-December

Zones: 100 Series (Northeast), 200 Series (Central), 300 Series (Southeast)

Early Antlerless Season

  • Dates: Typically mid-October (4-day season, select permit areas only)
  • Purpose: Allows harvest of antlerless deer to manage population.
  • Requirements: Must have an antlerless permit for that area.

License, Clothing, & Equipment Requirements

License Requirements

  • Firearm Deer: Resident Deer License (Standard or Bonus Antlerless).
  • Archery Deer: Resident Archery Deer License.
  • Muzzleloader: Muzzleloader Deer License (separate tag required).
  • Youth: Youth Deer License.
  • Waterfowl: Small Game License + State Duck Stamp + Federal Duck Stamp (age 16+).
  • Upland Birds: Small Game License only.

Clothing Requirements

  • Firearm Deer Season: Minimum of 50% blaze orange or pink outer clothing visible above the waist (hat, vest, or jacket).
  • Small Game / Upland Bird: Blaze orange hat or vest recommended for safety.
  • Archery Deer: Blaze orange/pink required during overlapping firearm seasons.

Firearm & Ammunition Limits

  • Shotgun (Waterfowl): Maximum of 3 shells (including one in the chamber).
  • Rifle (Deer): No magazine capacity limit, but must comply with centerfire restrictions.
  • Archery: Broadheads required; explosive or poisoned arrows prohibited.

Hunter Orange & Safety Notes

  • Always wear visible blaze orange/pink during any firearm deer season in the field.
  • Crossbows are legal for all hunters during archery season (2023 law update).
  • Check MN DNR Hunting Regulations for yearly updates.
